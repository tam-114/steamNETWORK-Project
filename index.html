WHAT IT IS

Product

steamNETWORK is a blog-based platform catered to students having another resource into figuring out their prospective careers utilizing: communication. The purpose of steamNETWORK is to facilitate connections and close the gap between what to expect and how to get there when pursuing Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics (STEAM) careers.

The idea is for students to have the opportunity to learn more from Experts in careers that they’re interested in. This is an entirely online experience, with different features to encourage engagement and new content to explore frequently. This is primarily Florida-based to uplift members of the community.

The key to the success of the steamNETWORK platform is through volunteer Experts that can share their experiences and journeys and facilitating engagement with students and Educators.

Main features are open to everyone and depending on users’ accounts there are different features available for use and communication. They are categorized by Experts, Students, Educators, and eventually Organizations.

Main Features

Discussion Forums: Forums are available for users to submit questions as topics with a title, and they're able to explain in more detail in the body text. Users can interact with the post and give their insight to questions, saving/bookmarking the discussion, and sharing it. Posts by the original user are included on their profile, as well as comments that are made on any post (similar to Reddit or Quora).

Blog Posts Comments: Experts can volunteer or be reached out to to be asked to conduct an “interview”. A member of the steamNETWORK team asks curated questions to the Expert and creates blog posts of their answers. These blog posts introduce the Expert and can be up to 10 or more questions at a time or it can be 3-5 open-ended questions. This is to give insight into their experiences with their career and answer frequently asked questions.

Attending Live Webinars: Live webinars are conducted with a member of the steamNETWORK team that allow for live attendance from interested users as a Q&A segment from Experts. Individual students are welcomed to attend, as well as Educators that request a speaker for specific topics and real-life applications. Webcams/microphones are encouraged for engagement, but steamNETWORK team members may also mute attendees and/or disable webcams for inappropriate behaviors that can result in an account ban.

Career Interest Assessments: Career Interest Assessments can be in-house applications that give insightful questions to provide a range of possible career opportunities that the user may be interested in. There are also many resources available, and the assessment feature may be a curated list of resources for users to take assessments, such as Career One Stop powered by O*NET Interest Profiler.

News Feed: The news feed shows partner organizations' up-coming events and conferences as organizations are able to upload their flyers/create text-posts and interact with the community on their post. It will also list volunteer opportunities available, and programs that encourage career growth such as internships, mentorships, and job shadowing opportunities in Florida.

WHAT IT COSTS

Budget

The majority of funding will come from grants and donations, as this would be a non-profit organization. There are no costs to sign-up for users or utilize the services. Another aspect could be from selling advertisement spacing on the platform.

WHAT IS USED

Tools

A breakdown of the tools to be used to create and maintain the presence of steamNETWORK.

Hosting

The web host, Bluehost, will be used for maintaining the site and making it accessible on the World Wide Web as it’s created with WordPress.org, it is also a recommended partner. It’s suitable for having unmetered bandwidth, large website storage space, and SSL certification included.

  • Bluehost
  • $180/year

Plugins

A WordPress plugin is a piece of software that “plugs into” the WordPress site. Plugins can add new functionality or extend existing functionality on the site, such as a membership feature, SEO capabilities, Mailchimp for WordPress, and so on. These are typically at a monthly cost.

  • WordPress
  • $230/year

Webinars

Webinars are live video feeds that allow for engagement with the audience, whether that’s with allowing questions, video/microphone capabilities from attendees, etc. The product to use for this feature will be Zoom, as they have plans available for recording sessions, changing permissions with attendees, and ease of use for moderators/hosts.

  • Zoom
  • $180/year

Email Management

Email management refers mostly to Newsletters and targeted email campaigns. Mailchimp is a great tool for creating newsletters and sending them en masse, as well as creating campaigns with analytics around the successes of it’s delivery. There’s a breakdown of clicks, how many people have opened it, who has opened it, whether there are unresponsive emails or bounce backs, etc. Email correspondence could also be handled through Mailchimp.

  • MailChimp
  • $200/year

Social Media Management

For social media management, the tool chosen was Buffer. This is because it acts as a central platform for creating posts as it also pairs with Canva, scheduling posts, creating drafts, and funding a plan for creating a social media presence for steamNETWORK. Social media will also be an important aspect as it is an online, social platform and spreading the word will most likely be through social media.

  • Buffer
  • $60/year
WHY ITS IMPORTANT

Background

This concept is important because it gives an opportunity for students to make more informed decisions about what they’d like to pursue. It doesn’t replace efforts like internships, mentorship programs, or job shadowing, but it can act as a resource for adding to those efforts and initiatives. For students that want to do more in-depth research into how to work towards their prospective careers and learn more about the ins and outs of the field they’d like to pursue.

My current role is as an intern for stemCONNET as a Workforce Development Intern. This is a child program of the Florida High Tech Corridor, which aims to inspire students to pursue STEM careers from K-12. We do that by hosting virtual connections between classrooms and Experts, where Educators can request for speakers to talk to their classes and give presentations to go more in-depth into topics and give real-life applications, and/or talk about their career journeys and educational paths. This lets students have the chance to ask questions to learn more about different types of careers or to understand a topic better. There are aspects in this project that include those things, but this platform gives more of an asynchronous communication style and not as much of a live webinar focus, with more individual opportunities to explore options, research, and continuous engagement.

I’d like to create a platform like this because I’ve joined many groups and organizations, yet rarely have the chance to speak with Experts and professionals about their paths and ask questions that may help others in a questioning situation. Experts can talk about how to network, how to job search in this day and age, give advice on how to stand out in an interview and much more. I have a passion for web design/development, and with my current internship I’ve grown much more of an interest in helping to aid with more educational resources. This platform would hope to serve as a central location for answers from students that aren’t sure what they want to pursue, and the ones that are 100% sure and want to learn more about what’s to come.

HOW TO GET THERE

Project Timeline

  • Starting Operations | Creating the LLC

    Jan - Feb 2023

    It can take a couple of weeks to register the company stemNETWORK with an LLC 501(c)(3) and decide on the main features of the site and what type of theme it will have, such as the color scheme, branding, logo, etc. It will also help to have research from similar organizations that promote STEM careers and for social networking.

  • Developing the Site

    Feb - Apr 2023

    To develop the site, it can take about 2 months with around the clock programming while maintaining a work/life balance. The steamNETWORK.com domain will be obtained and the right hosting service selected that can allow for straightforward upgrading for storage space when there’s more users to the site.

  • Social Media Presence

    Apr - May 2023

    Once the site is coming along, around the same time that the domain name is selected, then social media accounts will also be locked in. For example @steamNETWORK will be the consistent name across multiple social media platforms. These would include Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, etc. These would be established through the month.

  • Grant Applications

    May - Jun 2023

    Through the creation of steamNETWORK, the team will also be applying for grant applications for payments towards the tools used for the site. When there’s a foundation in place, there will also be a kickstarter started to help with the efforts of raising money. It is to be a non-profit limited liability company that mostly operates through grants and fundraising. Donations would be welcomed, but not as a primary form of funding.

  • Team Members | Expert Recruitment | Organization Partnerships

    Jun - Jul 2023

    As steamNETWORK solidifies, the team will expand for more members. These positions can include developers, moderators/recruiters, content writers, social media marketers, etc. The moderators/recruiters role would be to maintain users are following the etiquette of the site, meaning removing uncivil remarks and banning inappropriate behaviors from live webinars, discussions, etc. The moderators will host live webinars, and monitor discussion forums and blog comments. The recruiters will be in charge of outreach for bringing more Experts to the site, and working on creating more organization partnerships. Content writers will be interviewing Experts for blog content, and social media marketers will promote the site and maintain outreach efforts. This can take about a month with interviewing top applicants and onboarding.

  • Student/Educator Engagement

    Jul - Aug 2023

    Another key aspect of the site will be engagement from students and Educators. Once more Experts become users on the site and content is created with their blogs and facilitating conversation, we will outreach to more students and Educators to build the userbase. It will mainly be via social media, or partnerships with districts to encourage the use of steamNETWORK through schools.
